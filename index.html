About Me

Personal Information

Hola, I'm Miguel! I am a M.Sc. student in Data Science at the Sapienza University of Rome and a Data Scientist at Vivanta. I am very passionate about Social Networks and Data-Driven Modeling of Complex Phenomena. I am currently interested on several areas of research such as Opinion Dynamics, Information Diffusion, Inequality on Networks, Mobility Patterns, Graph Neural Networks and other interesting stuff.

In the past few years I completed my B.Sc. in Physics at the National Autonomous University of Mexico (UNAM) and completed an extra-curricular research internship at the Complex Systems Institute at Ghent University in Belgium studying the dynamics of opinion formation in Social Networks.

In my spare time I enjoy watching almost any sport and playing Squash. When I was younger I used to represent my country in international tournaments and I was part of the Mexican National Team.

My Name

My full name consists of 4 names: 2 first names (Miguel Ángel) and 2 last names (Sánchez Cortés). This is common in Latin American countries (such as Mexico, my home country) and Spain. In future publications, you will find me as Miguel Á. Sánchez-Cortés in order to avoid confusion with too many names. For personal matters, you can call me Miguel!

Intern at the Complex Systems Institute

Ghent University, Belgium
September 2021 - December 2021

3 month research internship at the Complex Systems Institute at Ghent University on Network Science focused on Social Networks, Community Analysis and Multilayer Networks. The project was used as an undergraduate thesis.

Main topic of the project: Analyzing data of the Eurovision Song Contest and building the voting network to perform community analysis and to simulate the voting mechanism using a Weighted Multilayer Network model.

Intern at the Dept. of Quantum Physics & Photonics

Institute of Physics, National Autonomous University of Mexico
August 2020 - June 2021

Undergraduate professional practices at the Institute of Physics of the National Autonomous University of Mexico focused in obtaining research experience on concepts like Rank Diversity, Power Laws, Probability Density Functions and Critical Phenomena on Natural & Social Sciences.

Main topic of the project: Simulating the Rank Dynamics of an urn with n balls using a Random Walk-like model and implementing a theoretical framework to further analyze and fit the model to real data.

Intern at the Dept. of Complex Systems

Institute of Physics, National Autonomous University of Mexico
January 2019 - January 2020

Undergraduate opportunity financed by the National Council of Science and Technology (CONACYT) at the Institute of Physics of the National Autonomous University of Mexico. The main objective of the internship was learning and obtaining research experience on the Theory of Complex Systems, Zipf-like Power Laws and the applications of Rank-Ordering Statistics in Natural Phenomena like earthquakes and hurricanes.
